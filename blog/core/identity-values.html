Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make.

In my work, I deal with many government contracts in Indonesia. A couple of years ago, we were the leading contender for a project until a very powerful government official approached us to request bribe money for us to secure the project. This person’s request was unreasonable and unethical. I don’t believe in discounting our own value and capabilities in winning the projects. I said no.

As a consequence, our chances diminished significantly. We came under constant attack and this official pushed for our competitor. I remember telling my team that we’re going to “science the shit out of this”. We decided we were going to double down on proving our technical and project capabilities.

In the final stages of the project, our proposal and detailed technical presentation were superior by a huge margin that the decision makers were convinced we were the only one in the country who could take on and deliver the project. This was a lesson for my team about the importance of building real and provable intrinsic capabilities and values in our company.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I am a third culture kid. That means I spent my formative years split between Indonesia, Singapore, New Zealand, the US, and Australia. It wasn’t easy being away from home and my parents since young. But that shaped me in both the good and the bad.

Growing up as a minority in all these countries gave me an undying fighting spirit. I know how to survive and hustle. I think it’s beautiful that growing up this way has led me to be innately incapable of differentiating people based on color, race, religion, or beliefs; rather for their character, values, vision, spirit, impact. I have been so blessed to have so many friends from all corners of the world through my upbringing and travels.

Of course, it was hard being on the road on my own. I had insufficient parental guidance and had to rely on street-smarts to adapt and assimilate. That led me to many wrong paths that I wasn’t smart enough not to take. Past trauma, unresolved pain, and pathological loneliness shaped a big part of me until recent years. It was only after I got sober that I could start healing.

Being of Chinese-Indonesian heritage also has its good and bad impacts. Positively, the business-oriented upbringing of a Chinese-Indonesian, has led me to understand entrepreneurship more intuitively. However, my culture has a lot of intergenerational pains being passed down. These came from our people’s need to survive in foreign countries. I am trying to break that chain of pain when it comes to my own children.

Lastly and more importantly, for me, in my journey, at the end of the day, all roads lead back to my faith in Jesus Christ.

An example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

Two of my teams at different companies had a recent conflict. They were blaming one another silently. And that led to back and forth communication and paperwork that weren’t getting things done. As a result, we were in danger of not delivering the products to the client.

Solutions were drawn, progress was made and now we are back on track in delivering on the outcome. The strategy and action were on point for this situation. But looking back, this situation will not happen if we had spent enough time in the past clarifying our expectations and process better. Work is ongoing to improve on this front.

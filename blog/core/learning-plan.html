Core Blog & Reflections:

Learning Plan

2 July 2022

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I started my career in Finance with Bloomberg LP until I left the company in 2008 to complete my postgraduate study (MBA). Since then, I have been an entrepreneur for the last 13 years. For now, this remains to be my career path - to build ventures. However, I have to admit, the emotional roller coaster of entrepreneurship has started to take a toll on me.

That said, right now, I am going to learn how to be a developer first. This is the right thing to do (for me) before I attempt any new venture in technology. However, due to my skills and experience in business, it is likely my long-term path will remain in the business or commercial side of things; or at least a hybrid role like a management position in a technology company.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

My number 1 strength in learning is my beginner’s mindset. I don’t mind to be “dumb” and I have learnt to say I don’t know. This gives me open mindedness and genuine curiosity. I am also a strong pattern recognizer and strategizer. I am able to see the big picture, ask critical questions, and carry intellectual conversations on different topics of interest. I can be in “flow” for a long-time if I am really interested in something. I am also an audio-visual learner who learns best from talks, presentations and peer-to-peer learning.

My number 1 weakness in learning is impatience and taking shortcuts when it comes to the details. Sometimes with a small knowledge of something, I would jump into conclusions without really going to the heart of the matter. My reliance toward data is inconsistent and often my interpretation would be biased in favor of my existing world view. My big-picture thinking can sometimes prevent me from thinking logically or in systematic order.

For a long time, I have also struggled with mental health and thus depressive episodes can translate into procrastination. I need deadlines and accountability. Lastly, I don’t have the patience for lengthy reading materials.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to be better at discipline and time management. I lost my productive rhythm during Covid times and I have been struggling to get it back.

I also want to develop more endurance and persistence. I have a stake in a coding bootcamp in Indonesia so I know these programs are designed to push us to break our own limits. I am anticipating some rough times ahead, but I want to see this through.

Lastly, in my last tech startup (that folded under Covid economic pressure), my development team consistently argued that I didn’t understand the “development culture”, the stress, and time it takes to do things. I want to understand this culture.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I have informed my work team members and my family members of my time commitment to this program. I have also blocked out August and September from any overseas travel or conference commitments. I am going to be there on-campus doing this program (with the exception of the two days that I mentioned earlier to Jared). I have genuine trust and respect for others, but I also know how to protect my barriers when needed. I expect to be on the ‘older-end’ of my cohort. My partying days are over. I am here to work and to really learn how to become a developer.

I am sure things will build up and will get to me at some point. My plan is to be authentic and real; to say things as they are. I will reach out for help and I will cry in front of you if I need to. I have learnt to be better with vulnerability.

Even at this stage of the program, I can feel this internal voice of “oh shit these guys are better than me”. I am acknowledging and accepting these feelings. However, I also understand that I will not make progress if I am stuck with not asking for help.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will ask for help. I know I will need help.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I am coming into the program as an “older” student. I am nervous at being slow and overwhelmed by the contents in the coming days. If I don’t get it, please be patient with me. I am committed to getting it. From time to time, I may need some hand holding.

I expect my facilitators to tell me as it is. Tell me if I am slacking and procrastinating; and encourage me to pick up the pace. I expect (and will need help) to be accountable for the deadlines; especially if I miss a few already. Please let me know if my own worldviews and past experience as an entrepreneur get in the way of my learning or if I am being arrogant.

Please be aware that I am someone who lives in recovery. This means I have a chronic struggle with mental health and my energy levels (and mood) will reflect these bouts. Ask me how I am doing if you see me having the blues.
